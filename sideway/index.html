A Bit About Me

Here's where the challenge presents itself. How am I going to convince you that I have what it takes to be your first hire? I feel like I should start from the beginning, with the things I can't put on my résumé.

With no prior programming knowledge, I taught myself Objective-C and was able to build a social networking app, SiddyTalk, using PHP and MySQL as it's backend. With the little experience I had, this was no easy feat. However, I was determined because I wanted to make this app. For example, it took me two weeks to learn how to get data from my API to a UITableView. Four months later, SiddyTalk was on the App Store for friends to download. Unfortunately, due to the fact that I attended school, I had to take down SiddyTalk because it was too much to handle. Though some people may consider SiddyTalk a failure, I see it as a humble beginning. My mentality is, “If I can build a social network with no prior programming skills, then I can build anything”. And that is exactly what I have done.

Since then, I’ve taken numerous computer science classes to expand my knowledge in programming as well as taking on more personal projects. I have brought in an array of programming languages, full-stack development, hardware development, Machine Learning, even tools to help me design API's, e.g. CodeRED API Design.

What motivates you?

There are few things that motivate me to work as hard as I do, and that is my curiosity and the challenge of a project. For instance, I was very curious about Machine Learning, so I decided to take it up this summer. Man am I glad I did! Machine Learning has been the biggest challenge since my early days of programming. I honestly enjoy the challenge of not knowing what to do because I know with time, I will not only get it, I will master it. Knowing that pushes me to continue to learn new technologies even when I feel I am not qualified to learn it.

Why this job?

It’s actually quite simple, given the opportunity to work with Sideway, I would be able to use my creative mind to help build a product. I don't mind sitting behind a desk all day, as long as I get to build some cool shit while I'm at it.

What does your perfect environment look like?

My perfect environment...hmmmm, I feel most comfortable working in my home office. I get my best work done during the night where distractions such as text messages or social media cannot disrupt my concentration.

My current work schedule(Central Time):

  • 10pm-5am - Work.
  • 5am-10am - Sleep.
  • 10am-5pm - Work.
  • 5pm-10pm - Relax, go run, sleep etc.

Got a front end stack in mind? What is it?

I feel this question could be a double edged sword. I have a lot of experience with the MEAN STACK, but that’s not to say that Angular is the best choice for Sideway. I need to have a more in-depth understanding of Sideway before recommending a framework. Ultimately, it is not my decision to make, it’s for the team to decide. I have a strong understanding of MVC, so regardless if I have experience with the chosen framework or not, I feel very confident in my abilities to develop the front end.

What besides writing code are you passionate about?

Besides coding, another one of my passions is basketball. I even get my competitive nature from it. Basketball has taught me “It takes a good team with great cohesion in order to win, not just one great player”. In our case, a rockstar developer stands no chance against a team of good developers that trust and work well with each other. I see this all the time at hackathons.

Without getting too philosophical, the second thing I am passionate about is gaining more knowledge. Whether that's learning from those who are more experienced than me, taking free courses on Coursera, or watching YouTube videos over random things like Science Of Persuasion, knowledge to me is the most valuable thing a human can possess.
